UniGenDet: A Unified Generative-Discriminative Framework for Co-Evolutionary Image Generation and Generated Image Detection

Department of Automation, Tsinghua University, China

CVPR 2026

Fig. 1. UniGenDet bridges generation and detection in a unified, co-evolutionary framework.

UniGenDet unifies image generation and generated-image detection into a single co-evolving framework. Instead of treating generation and detection as isolated problems, we connect them with detector-informed signals so that the generator aligns toward authenticity while the detector becomes more robust to evolving generative artifacts.

Key Idea

Focus on visual evidence; keep metrics brief.

  • Unified co-evolution: generation and detection are jointly optimized rather than separately tuned.
  • Detector-informed alignment: use discriminative cues to guide generative learning toward more authentic outputs.
  • Robust detection: co-evolution exposes the detector to harder, evolving negatives to improve generalization.

This page highlights the methodology and qualitative visualizations. Detailed numeric settings and extended ablations are left to the paper.

Method Overview

Fig. 2. Generation–Detection Unified Fine-tuning (GDUF): the high-level co-evolution loop.
Fig. 3. Detector-Informed Generative Alignment (DIGA): inject detector-aware signals for generative alignment.

At a high level, UniGenDet alternates between improving the generator and strengthening the detector under a unified objective. DIGA serves as the bridge: discriminative signals and artifact-sensitive cues are fed back to the generator, making the generated samples progressively more realistic while simultaneously creating harder examples for detection.
